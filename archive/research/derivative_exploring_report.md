# Part 1: TradeFi Vendors in Crypto Derivatives

## 1. What is "TradeFi" in the Crypto Derivatives Context?

**TradeFi** (Traditional Finance) in the crypto derivatives context refers to the adaptation, integration, or direct transplantation of established financial market infrastructure -- originally built for equities, commodities, fixed income, and FX derivatives -- into the cryptocurrency and digital asset ecosystem.

This encompasses:

- **Infrastructure Migration:** Traditional matching engines, OMS, EMS, risk management, clearing and settlement systems adapted for crypto-native assets (perpetual futures, crypto options, tokenized derivatives)
- **Institutional Standards:** Regulatory compliance frameworks, audit trails, KYC/AML, credit risk management, portfolio margining
- **Product Convergence:** Vanilla/exotic options, structured notes with ISINs, basis trades, variance swaps on crypto underlyings; crypto exchanges offering derivatives on traditional assets

**Key Statistics (2025):**
- CME Group: ~$3 trillion notional volume in crypto derivatives
- Q4 2025: 92% ADV increase, >100% OI increase YoY at CME
- Record 1,039 large open interest holders (Oct 2025)
- Coinbase acquired Deribit for ~$2.9 billion

# Part 2: Prediction Markets

## 1. What Are Prediction Markets?

Prediction markets are exchange-traded platforms where participants trade contracts tied to future real-world event outcomes. Contracts price between $0.00-$1.00, reflecting aggregate probability estimates. They function as **event derivatives** -- financial contracts whose value derives from event outcomes rather than asset prices.

### Market Types
- **Binary:** Yes/No (e.g., "Will BTC exceed $150K by June 2026?")
- **Scalar:** Range settlement (e.g., "What will CPI be in Q3 2026?")
- **Categorical:** Multiple discrete outcomes (e.g., "Who wins the 2026 World Cup?")

## 3. Prediction Markets as Derivatives

| Feature | Prediction Markets | Binary Options | Options | Futures |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|---------|---------|
| Underlying | Real-world event | Asset price/event | Asset price | Asset price |
| Payoff | Binary (0/1) | Binary | Variable | Variable |
| Max Loss | Contract price | Fixed stake | Premium (buyer) | Theoretically unlimited |
| Margin | Full collateral | Full collateral | Margin for writers | Margin required |

**Key insight:** Prediction markets are functionally equivalent to binary options tied to real-world events, with market-driven pricing rather than broker-set pricing.
